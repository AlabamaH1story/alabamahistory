Early History

Alabama joined the union as the 22cd state in 1819. It is lacated in the southeastern United States and nicknamed "The Heart of Dixie", "The Cotton State", or "The Yellowhammer State". The region that later became Alabama was occupied by aboriginals as early as 10,000 years ago. Europeans found the area in the 16th century. During the first half of the 19th century cotton and slave labor were important to Alabama's economy. The state was important to the Confederacy because Montgomery, Alabama's Capital, was the Confederacy's first capital. By the early 21st century, the states economy was based on agriculture, auto production, and the service sector.
